Psyche's believed origin

Scientists think asteroid Psyche could be part or all of the iron-rich core of a planetesimal, a building block of a rocky planet.

How did Psyche get its name

Psyche was discovered in 1852 by Italian astronomer Annibale de Gasparis. Because it was the 16th asteroid to be discovered, it is sometimes referred to as 16 Psyche. It’s named for the goddess of the soul in ancient Greek mythology, often depicted as a butterfly-winged female figure.

Location in our Solar System

Psyche is located in between Mars and Jupiter, and because Psyche and Earth orbit at different speeds, the distance from Earth to Psyche varies from less than 186 million miles to more than 372 million miles

A day on Psyche

A day on Psyche is not like a day here on Earth. Shorter days, longer years, and a lower amount of birthdays

Weather on Psyche

You are going to need your coat, amongst other things. Psyche's temperatures are believed to range from around -100°F (-70°C) to -340°F (-200°C)

How physics differs on Psyche

Have you ever seen a strong man competition and wished you too could pick up a car? Well today is your lucky day! If you can lift your dog up on Earth, you most likely can lift your family's car up on Psyche!

Psyche's Surface

Psyche's surface is believed to very greatly, as radar technology has provided us with what is believed to be many small craters and two very large craters.
